The Operating Philosophy

Engaged partners build pipeline. Pipeline doesn't build engaged partners.

In a partner build motion, the leading indicators are not revenue — they're participation. A partner actively engaged in joint business planning, co-marketing, and governance cadences will generate pipeline. A partner sitting on a roster without engagement will not — regardless of their tier or certification status. This framework was built to measure what actually predicts partner success, intervene early when signals deteriorate, and protect ecosystem ROI before pipeline impact becomes visible.
01
Partner Segmentation — The Foundation

Segmentation happens before health scoring. A partner's technology capability, skill level, and certification status determines account assignment eligibility, enablement investment priority, and how health scoring weights are applied. Getting segmentation right is the precondition for everything else.

Step 1
Partner Type

Classify by go-to-market motion and primary business model. Determines co-sell approach and governance cadence.

Step 2
Technology Mapping

Map each partner to active technology categories. Partners may operate across multiple categories at different capability levels.

Step 3
Skill & Cert Assessment

Assess technical skill level (org-level) and certification status per technology category. Updated each cycle.

Step 4
Profile → Score

Segmentation profile feeds directly into health scoring, account assignment, and MDF allocation decisions.

Partner Type Definitions

MSSP
Managed Security Service Provider
Security is the primary business. Dedicated SOC, 24/7 threat monitoring, incident response. Highest GTM fit for security portfolio co-sell. Engaged from day one if pipeline model is right.
MSP
Managed Service Provider
Broad IT services — infrastructure, cloud, helpdesk. Security is an expansion play, not core. Requires higher enablement investment. Pipeline follows capability build, not the other way around.
ISV
Independent Software Vendor
Technology integration partners. Co-sell motion centers on solution bundling and joint value proposition. Pipeline driven by technical integration depth and joint customer base.
OEM
Original Equipment Manufacturer
Embed or bundle vendor technology into their own product. High-value, high-complexity partnerships. Pipeline metrics operate on different timelines than resell or co-sell partners.
BP
Business Partner / Reseller
Traditional channel motion — sell-through and co-sell. Pipeline is the primary health signal. Enablement investment directly correlates with quota attainment speed.
Key Distinction MSSPs enter the ecosystem security-ready. MSPs enter IT-ready and build toward security. Health scoring weights reflect this — MSSPs score faster on Pipeline Activity; MSPs score faster on Workshop & JBP Engagement first, then pipeline follows as capability builds.

Workshop, Training & JBP Engagement
Participation in vendor-led workshops, training sessions, and joint business planning activities
✓ Healthy

Actively attends workshops and training; JBP in place with current targets and milestones; exec sponsor engaged in joint planning

⚠ At Risk

Attending some sessions but not all; JBP exists but not actively reviewed; exec sponsor disengaged from planning sessions

✗ Critical

Not participating in workshops or training; no active JBP; joint planning sessions missed or declined for 2+ cycles

25
QBR & Governance Cadence
Attendance and engagement quality in quarterly business reviews and governance calls
✓ Healthy

100% QBR attendance; executive sponsor present; action items completed between reviews; strategic conversations, not status updates

⚠ At Risk

Missed 1 QBR; operational-level attendance only (no exec sponsor); action item follow-through inconsistent

✗ Critical

Missed 2+ QBRs; no executive engagement; non-responsive to governance cadence; calls rescheduled repeatedly

20
Co-Marketing & Campaign Planning Participation
Engagement in joint campaigns, MDF utilization, webinars, field events, and campaign planning sessions
✓ Healthy

Active in 2+ co-marketing programs per quarter; MDF fully utilized; engaged in campaign planning; contributing ideas and resources

⚠ At Risk

Participating in 1 program; partial MDF utilization; attending but not contributing to campaign planning

✗ Critical

Zero co-marketing participation; MDF unused; not responding to campaign planning outreach

15
Pipeline Activity
Active opportunities, deal velocity, and pipeline contribution vs. quarterly target — a lagging indicator that follows engagement
✓ Healthy

At or above quarterly pipeline target; new opportunities added monthly; deal velocity on track; partner-sourced opps converting

⚠ At Risk

Pipeline 50–75% of target; opportunities stagnant; no new deals in 30 days; velocity slowing

✗ Critical

Below 50% of target; zero new opportunities in 60+ days; existing pipeline stalled with no movement

Note: Pipeline is weighted lower by design. In a build motion, a partner with strong JBP, QBR, and co-marketing engagement but developing pipeline is healthier than a partner with one recent deal and zero engagement. Pipeline follows — it does not lead.
10
Platform Adoption
Active usage of platform tools, portal, and partner-facing resources per technology category
✓ Healthy

Regular portal usage; 80%+ feature adoption; using partner-facing tools independently without prompting

⚠ At Risk

Logging in but not utilizing key features; using 1–2 tools only; requires prompting to engage with platform resources

✗ Critical

No login in 30+ days; zero tool usage; portal access not activated or abandoned after onboarding

5
Performance & Satisfaction
Internal team assessment of partner engagement quality, contribution, and working relationship — scored by the partner management team, not self-reported
✓ Healthy (8–10)

Proactively participating and contributing — brings ideas, engages constructively, advocates for the program internally

⚠ At Risk (6–7)

Passive participant — responsive but not proactive; expressing friction without escalating constructively

✗ Critical (<6)

Poor working relationship — unresponsive, adversarial, or actively disengaged from program objectives

04
Case Study — Early Detection in Practice
Anonymized · IBM Partner Ecosystem · Data & AI Portfolio
MSSP Partner: Score drop caught before pipeline impact — recovered within 45 days
Situation

Routine quarterly scoring flagged a Tier 1 MSSP dropping from 84 to 61. Revenue pipeline still showed $240K active. Without the health score, nothing would have triggered a review for another quarter.

Root Cause & Intervention

Platform adoption had dropped to zero — a product update had broken their integration workflow. Discovery call in Week 2 surfaced the issue. Escalated to Product team; resolved within 10 days. New QBP agreed with exec sponsor re-engaged.

Outcome

Health score recovered to 79 within 45 days. Existing $240K pipeline closed. Two new opportunities added in the following quarter. Partner became a reference for the program.

Model Evolution & Implementation

Originally designed in Microsoft Excel to manage a 300+ partner ecosystem at IBM — before CRM workflow automation was available at the required scale. Upgraded to CRM/PRM as tooling matured, enabling automation of alerts, dashboards, and QBR prep reporting that previously required manual assembly.

  • Excel (origin) — Partner segmentation matrix (50 partners × 50+ products), manual quarterly scoring per dimension, pivot table reporting, conditional formatting for tier classification and at-risk flagging
  • CRM/PRM (upgraded) — Custom partner account fields per dimension, threshold-triggered alerts, real-time health leaderboard, QBR prep auto-generation, renewal forecasting weighted by health score
  • Scoring cadence — Quarterly assessment by the internal partner management team. Segmentation profile updated when a partner advances certification or enters a new technology category
  • Account assignment gate — Segmentation profile (skill level + certification status per technology) determines account assignment eligibility. Partners with Low skill or uncertified status are not assigned accounts in that technology until profile improves
