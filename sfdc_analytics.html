SFDC Partner Analytics

Using Salesforce pipeline data to identify MSSP churn risk, deploy GTM interventions, and optimize ecosystem performance
AuthorC. Johnson, MBA
PlatformSalesforce CRM/PRM
EcosystemMSSP · MSP · GSI · VAR
DateApril 2026
This document describes the Salesforce-based partner analytics approach developed across IBM's North America GTM organization (300+ partners: MSSPs, ISVs, OEMs, and Business Partners) and subsequently applied to Google's GCP partner program via Denave (MSSP-focused network, under 100 partners). The methodology centers on using leading-indicator data to identify at-risk partners before pipeline impact is visible, and deploying targeted GTM interventions that measurably improve ecosystem health and revenue contribution. Applied to the Denave/GCP program, this approach delivered a 25% reduction in partner attrition and a 40% increase in platform activation among priority partners.

03
Key SFDC Reports & Dashboards
Partner Health Leaderboard

Real-time ranking of all partners by composite health score. Filters by partner type (MSSP, GSI, VAR), region, and tier. Updated automatically as SFDC data refreshes.

  • Color-coded by tier (green / amber / red)
  • Drill-down to dimension-level breakdown per partner
  • 30/60/90-day trend line per partner
  • At-Risk flag with days since alert triggered
Pipeline Velocity Tracker

Monitors opportunity creation rate, stage progression, and deal velocity per partner. Leading indicator for revenue impact before deals close or stall.

  • New opportunities created (last 30 days) per partner
  • Average days in stage — flags stalled deals
  • Partner pipeline vs. quarterly target (% attainment)
  • Win rate by partner type and segment
Renewal Forecasting Model

Combines health score, contract end date, and pipeline activity to produce a renewal probability score for each partner relationship.

  • Renewal probability: High / Medium / Low / At Risk
  • 90-day pre-renewal alert for At Risk partners
  • Intervention trigger linked to renewal forecast
  • Historical renewal rate by health score tier
QBR Prep Report

Auto-generated partner report pulled before each quarterly business review. Gives partner managers and executives a complete picture of partner performance without manual data assembly.

  • 90-day health score trend
  • Dimension breakdown with flags
  • Pipeline summary and win rate
  • Open action items from last QBR
  • Co-marketing participation and MDF utilization
04
Case Example — MSSP Churn Prevention
Situation

During a routine weekly health score review, three MSSP partners dropped below the 70-point threshold simultaneously. Revenue data showed no impact yet — all three had active pipeline. But the health score flagged declining platform adoption and zero co-marketing participation for 45+ days.

PartnerHealth ScorePrimary Dimension DropPipeline StatusRisk
MSSP Partner A64Platform Adoption (↓18pts)Active — $240K pipelineAt Risk
MSSP Partner B58Co-Marketing Participation (↓15pts) + QBR Attendance (↓10pts)Stagnant — no new opps in 45 daysCritical
MSSP Partner C61Training Completion (↓12pts)Active — $180K pipelineAt Risk
Intervention & Outcome
  • Partner A — Discovery call revealed platform update had broken their integration workflow. Escalated to Product team, resolved within 2 weeks. Health score recovered to 81 within 30 days. Deal closed.
  • Partner B — Low engagement traced to internal reorganization. New executive sponsor identified and engaged. Recovery plan agreed. Health score improved to 72 within 45 days.
  • Partner C — Training backlog due to headcount change. Dedicated enablement session scheduled. Certifications current within 3 weeks. Health score recovered to 78.

Without the health scoring alert system, these three partners would likely have churned within 90 days — representing approximately $420K in at-risk pipeline and significant replacement cost.
